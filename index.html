Short Bio

Martin Schulz is a Full Professor and Chair for Computer Architecture & Parallel Systems at the Technical University of Munich (since 2017) and a member of the Board of Directors at the Leibniz Supercomputing Centre. Prior to that, he held positions at LLNL/CASC and Cornell University. He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from TUM (2001) and an M.S. from UIUC. His interests include parallel and distributed architectures and applications; performance analysis and modeling; memory system optimization; programming models and tools (Chair of the MPI Forum); energy‑aware and fault‑tolerant computing; and quantum computing with a focus on HPC/QC integration.
